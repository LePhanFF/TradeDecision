=== C01 - Market-Generated Information & Auction Basics (pp. 6-45) ===

[ID] C01-001 (Easy)
Q: Which pair best describes the two core auction modes Dalton emphasizes?
A) Breakout and breakdown
B) Momentum and volatility
C) Price discovery and value discovery
D) Reversal and continuation
Correct: C) Price discovery and value discovery
Why: Auctions explore to find new prices and then build acceptance into value.
Evidence: [MiP - Market-Generated Information & Auction Basics, pp. 6-45]

[ID] C01-002 (Easy)
Q: Why does Dalton favor market-generated information (MGI) over predictions?
A) Because it ignores context
B) Because MGI reflects real-time behavior you can update against
C) Because volume is all that matters
D) Because predictions are usually right
Correct: B) Because MGI reflects real-time behavior you can update against
Why: MGI is a live feedback loop from time, price, and structure that guides adaptation.
Evidence: [MiP - Market-Generated Information & Auction Basics, pp. 6-45]

[ID] C01-003 (Easy)
Q: Which observation is strongest evidence of acceptance at a price area?
A) A gap that fills immediately
B) A thin strip of single prints
C) Multiple overlapping 30-minute periods and time spent at the level
D) A single fast print through the level
Correct: C) Multiple overlapping 30-minute periods and time spent at the level
Why: Acceptance is signaled by time and overlap, not just a touch.
Evidence: [MiP - Market-Generated Information & Auction Basics, pp. 6-45]

[ID] C03-001 (Medium)

A) The overnight range
B) The session midpoint
C) The first 60 minutes' high-low range

Why: Dalton's IB frames the session for evaluating range extension.
Evidence: [MiP - Initial Balance, Range Extension, Attempted Direction, pp. 70-92]

=== C04 - Excess, Rejection, Single Prints, Poor Highs/Lows (pp. 103-157) ===

[ID] C04-001 (Easy)
Q: Which most clearly signals rejection at the high?
A) A slow grind with overlap
B) A prominent POC at the high
C) A long tail of single prints followed by rotation back
D) A flat multi-TPO top
Correct: C) A long tail of single prints followed by rotation back
Why: Single-print tails are classic excess marking an auction end at that extreme.
Evidence: [MiP - Excess, Rejection, Single Prints, Poor Highs/Lows, pp. 103-157]

[ID] C04-002 (Medium)
Q: A poor low implies:
A) The auction lacked excess and may be repaired later
B) Immediate trend day
C) A durable low not to be tested again
D) Irrelevance
Correct: A) The auction lacked excess and may be repaired later
